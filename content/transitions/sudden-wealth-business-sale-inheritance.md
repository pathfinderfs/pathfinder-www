An inheritance, business sale, legal settlement, or other liquidity event can bring relief and pressure at the same time. The right first step is often not action but structure. We help clients organize the moving pieces, create decision space, and build a durable plan before money begins drifting toward avoidable taxes, rushed investments, or commitments they did not fully intend to make.

## When this transition gets complicated

- Large balances arrive faster than a full plan for taxes, investing, gifting, or family requests.
- The emotional meaning of the money can make even routine decisions feel heavy.
- A business sale may create concentrated tax exposure and questions about what comes next.
- Existing investments or advisors may no longer fit the scale or purpose of the assets.

## How Pathfinder can help

- Help you establish a thoughtful pace for decisions after a liquidity event.
- Coordinate with your CPA and estate attorney on taxes, charitable giving, and estate considerations.
- Build an investment allocation that reflects both your new balance sheet and your broader life goals.
- Clarify how this new wealth should support spending, family, philanthropy, and future generations.

## Questions we often help clients answer

- How much of this should stay liquid while I decide what comes next?
- What taxes should I be preparing for now?
- How do I respond thoughtfully to loans, gifts, or financial requests from others?
- How can this money support the life I actually want to lead?
