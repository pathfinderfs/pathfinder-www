After the loss of a spouse, important financial tasks often arrive before there has been any real time to grieve. Accounts may need to be located, benefits claimed, titles changed, and income plans revised. We help clients organize the immediate priorities, reduce administrative burden where we can, and avoid making irreversible decisions before they are ready.

## When this transition gets complicated

- There may be many accounts, policies, and legal documents to gather and sort through.
- Household income can change quickly after the loss of a pension, paycheck, or Social Security benefit.
- Investment, housing, and estate decisions may feel urgent even when more time would be better.
- Adult children, other relatives, or outside advisors may all be involved at once.

## How Pathfinder can help

- Help organize the first layer of financial tasks and identify what needs attention first.
- Review cash flow changes and adjust the plan for a different income reality.
- Coordinate with estate attorneys, tax professionals, and family members when needed.
- Create space for thoughtful decisions about investing, housing, and legacy after the immediate crisis has passed.

## Questions we often help clients answer

- What needs to be handled now, and what can wait?
- How will my income change going forward?
- What do I need to do with accounts, beneficiaries, or required paperwork?
- When is the right time to revisit the long-term plan?
