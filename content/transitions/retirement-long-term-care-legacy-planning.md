Retirement changes the conversation from accumulation to durability. At the same time, questions about long-term care, family support, estate documents, and what you want to leave behind often become more immediate. We help clients think through those choices together so the plan works for both daily life and the future that follows.

## When this transition gets complicated

- Retirement income may need to last longer than expected if markets, inflation, or spending change.
- A spouse or parent may need care, and the cost, setting, and timing can affect the whole family.
- Medicare, supplemental coverage, and possible long-term care needs can create a lot of moving parts.
- Estate documents, beneficiary designations, and account titling may no longer match current wishes.
- Decisions about gifts, support for children, or charitable goals can conflict with the need to preserve flexibility.

## How Pathfinder can help

- Stress-test retirement spending and cash flow against different market and care-cost scenarios.
- Build a planning framework that addresses current lifestyle needs and longer-term legacy goals.
- Coordinate with attorneys, accountants, and insurance professionals on estate and care-related questions.
- Clarify how assets, beneficiary designations, and support for family members fit into the broader plan.
- Help you make decisions with enough structure to be deliberate without overcomplicating the process.

## Questions we often help clients answer

- How much can I safely spend without risking the rest of the plan?
- What happens if care costs arrive sooner or last longer than expected?
- How should I think about Medicare, long-term care coverage, or housing changes?
- What updates should I make to my estate plan and beneficiary designations?
- How do I balance present needs with the legacy I want to leave?
