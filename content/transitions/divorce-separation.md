Divorce is both emotional and administrative. In the middle of that strain, you may be making permanent decisions about housing, retirement accounts, taxes, and future cash flow. We help clients understand the long-term impact of those choices so important decisions are not made in a fog of urgency.

## When this transition gets complicated

- Settlement proposals can look fair on paper while leading to very different long-term outcomes.
- Dividing retirement accounts and taxable accounts requires attention to taxes, timing, and legal process.
- Housing decisions often carry emotional weight as well as financial consequences.
- A post-divorce budget may need to support a different lifestyle, a different income level, or new caregiving responsibilities.

## How Pathfinder can help

- Model the long-term tradeoffs behind proposed settlement structures.
- Help you evaluate asset division with attention to taxes, liquidity, and future flexibility.
- Coordinate with your attorney and accountant when QDROs or other technical details are involved.
- Build a new planning baseline around spending, savings, insurance, and investment management.

## Questions we often help clients answer

- Can I realistically afford to keep the house?
- How should retirement accounts be divided and transferred?
- Which assets are more useful to keep, even if their balances look similar?
- What does financial independence look like after the separation is complete?
